Section 01

Consumer Neurotechnology Landscape

The 2020s saw consumer brain-computer interface technology transition from laboratory curiosity to mass-market product. By 2026, an estimated 85 million consumer EEG, ERP-capable hearable, and neural-adjacent wearable devices are active globally — a 12× increase from 2020.

The dominant device categories span an invasive spectrum from surgically implanted electrode arrays to purely passive acoustic monitors:

🧠 Implanted

Cortical Arrays

Neuralink N1, Synchron Stentrode. Sub-millimetre electrode density; continuous broadband neural recording. Highest fidelity, highest risk surface.

🎧 Non-invasive wearable

Smart Hearables

Meta Ray-Ban Gen 4, Apple AirPods Pro (neural mode). In-ear EEG electrodes targeting alpha / theta via meatal contact. Sold as focus / wellbeing tools.

👓 Optical + EEG hybrid

AR Glasses

Google Glass XR / Android XR generation. Temple-contact dry electrodes, eye-tracking, micro-expression video. Fused neural signal.

⌚ Peripheral biomarker

Cognitive Wearables

Oura Ring 4, Samsung Galaxy Ring. HRV, GSR, sleep-stage classification. Indirect cognitive-state proxies via autonomic nervous system.

🔬 Consumer EEG headset

Dry-Electrode Headbands

Neurosity Crown 3, Muse S Gen 3. 8–16 channels, 256 Hz. Consumer-grade but sufficient for reliable P300 / ERN biometric extraction.

The commercial opportunity in neural data is substantial: McKinsey estimates the neurotechnology market will exceed $400 billion by 2030, with the largest revenue streams coming from cognitive advertising, productivity analytics, and insurance risk modelling — all categories that directly incentivise the exploitation of intimate neural signals.

Section 02

Legal & Regulatory Frameworks

The regulatory environment for neural data remains fragmented, with protections ranging from constitutional (Chile) to sector-specific statute (Colorado, California) to comprehensive AI governance (EU AI Act).

Chilean Neurorights Constitutional Amendment (2021)

The world's first constitutional neurorights framework. Article 19 §1 was amended to protect five rights: mental privacy, cognitive liberty, mental integrity, psychological continuity, and equal cognitive access. Any commercial reading or manipulation of neural signals without explicit consent is unconstitutional.

EU AI Act — Neurorights Provisions (2024–2025)

Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 classifies systems that infer emotions, psychological states, or perform real-time biometric identification as high-risk (Annex III) or prohibited (Art. 5). The proposed Oviedo Protocol extension (2025 draft) adds explicit neural-data protections equivalent to genetic data under GDPR Art. 9.

Colorado AI Act — SB 24-205 (effective 2026)

Treats neurotechnology used in consequential decisions (hiring, insurance, credit, healthcare triage) as high-risk AI. Developers must publish annual algorithmic impact assessments; deployers must provide opt-out and human-review pathways.

California

AB 1836 / SB 1223 (2024)

Classifies neural data as sensitive personal information under CCPA. Prohibits use for targeted advertising. Requires opt-in consent.

New York

Int. 1306-A (2025)

Prohibits commercial sale of neural interface data without explicit per-individual informed consent.

International

UNESCO AI Ethics (2024 Update)

Updated Recommendation includes neurobiometric data under binding guidance for member states; data minimisation and purpose limitation.

Section 03

Key Risks & Threat Model

Consumer neurotechnology introduces a threat landscape unlike any prior generation of personal devices. The signals are irrevocable (you cannot change your ERP latency), involuntary (neural responses occur below conscious control), and inference-rich (raw EEG encodes far more than the user intends to share).

Primary Risk Vectors

Identity

Neural Fingerprinting

P300 / N200 / ERN latency patterns constitute a biometric as unique as a fingerprint — and cannot be revoked if leaked.

Inference

Covert Cognitive Profiling

Emotion, political preference, religious belief, sexual orientation, and health status can be inferred from raw EEG without explicit disclosure.

Manipulation

Closed-Loop Influence

Real-time neural feedback enables sub-second content adaptation targeting individual reward and attention pathways.

Infrastructure

Neural Data Exfiltration

SDK caches, BLE sniffing, and cloud sync pipelines create exfiltration surfaces for retroactive neural profiling.

Section 04

Neurobiometrics

Neural biometrics are the most powerful — and most dangerous — identification technology ever deployed at consumer scale. Unlike all prior biometrics, they combine irrevocability with involuntary disclosure and inferential scope that extends far beyond identity.

Neurobiometric Risk Dimensions

Irrevocable

No Revocation Path

ERP latency fingerprints are stable across decades. A leaked neural biometric cannot be replaced like a password or key.

Cross-Platform

Re-Identification

Neural fingerprints extracted from one device (headset) can re-identify the same user on a different platform (BCI, hearable) across years.

Involuntary

Non-Consensual Disclosure

P300 responses and ERN signatures are elicited by external stimuli below conscious control — users cannot choose not to emit them.

Inference

Beyond Identity

Raw EEG encodes health, cognitive state, emotional valence, political orientation, and deception markers not disclosed by the user.

Defense

NeuralMask™

CognitiveCanary's EEGShield layer injects adversarial perturbations that preserve clinical utility while destroying biometric extractability.

Section 07

Cognitive Security & Attack Surfaces

Beyond legal compliance and biometric privacy, consumer neurotechnology opens an entirely new attack surface: the cognitive security domain, where adversaries can probe, manipulate, and exploit the human nervous system itself as an attack vector. This section enumerates the principal attack categories identified in 2025–2026 research.

7.1 — Neural Data in Transit

Consumer EEG devices transmit raw neural signals over BLE, Wi-Fi, and proprietary RF protocols — largely without the end-to-end encryption mandated for comparable medical devices.

CRITICAL
Device Pairing / BLE MITM — Key-Downgrade Attacks
Bluetooth LE pairing is vulnerable to key-downgrade attacks (BLESA, BIAS variants) that allow an attacker within RF range to intercept the full neural data stream during or after pairing. Consumer EEG headsets rarely implement authenticated key exchange; pairing typically relies on a proximity assumption that attackers can trivially violate with directional antennas.
HIGH
RF Injection — False Cognitive State Injection via SDR
Software-defined radio (SDR) equipment available for under $200 can replay or synthesise BLE/ANT+ packets carrying counterfeit neural data payloads. When injected into a closed-loop neurofeedback application, fabricated cognitive-state signals can drive the application to deliver adversarial interventions (audio, visual, or haptic) targeting the user's actual neural state.
HIGH
Adversarial ML Binary Poisoning — Edge Classifier Replacement
Consumer EEG devices increasingly run on-device ML classifiers (TFLite, ONNX) for real-time cognitive state inference. Firmware update mechanisms lacking cryptographic signing allow attackers to replace these models with poisoned versions that misclassify neutral states as actionable cognitive events, generating fraudulent neurofeedback or enabling covert profiling.
MODERATE
Persistence & Storage — SDK Cache Leakage, Retroactive Profiling
Manufacturer SDKs (and many third-party integrations) cache decoded cognitive features and ERP timestamps in local SQLite databases and cloud sync queues without expiry. These caches constitute a high-value retroactive profiling surface: an attacker gaining later access to the device or cloud account can reconstruct a longitudinal neural history extending years before the compromise.

7.2 — Reinforcement & Behavioral Conditioning

CRITICAL
Reward Pathway Hijacking — Closed-Loop Dopamine Targeting
Closed-loop neurofeedback systems that read EEG theta/alpha and modulate audio/visual rewards in real time can, when adversarially tuned, target the mesolimbic dopamine pathway directly. Reward delivery timed to endogenous theta bursts amplifies compulsive engagement loops indistinguishable from organic motivation — a precision addiction vector.
HIGH
Algorithmic Amplification — Sub-Second Neural Engagement Loops
Recommendation algorithms fed real-time EEG engagement metrics can operate on a sub-second feedback cycle — far faster than conscious awareness. This creates an asymmetric adversarial surface: the algorithm knows the user's engagement state before the user does, enabling content sequencing optimised to maintain flow states that maximise session time at the cost of autonomy.

7.3 — Synthetic Conversational Persuasion

HIGH
Adversarial LLMs & PII Leakage via BCI-Integrated Assistants
LLM-based assistants integrated with BCI data streams are vulnerable to prompt injection attacks that cause the assistant to exfiltrate neural context (cognitive state, ERP responses, attention patterns) to attacker-controlled endpoints. The combination of language model reasoning and neural-state awareness creates a manipulation surface with no historical precedent.
CRITICAL
Neural Adaptive Persuasion — Cognitive Vulnerability Profiling
By combining longitudinal EEG data (attention, cognitive load, emotional arousal) with ML models trained on persuasion research, adversarial systems can identify individual cognitive vulnerability windows — moments when the target is fatigued, emotionally activated, or in a low-resistance state — and time persuasive interventions to coincide with maximum susceptibility.

7.4 — Neurophysiology Exploitation

HIGH
Sensory Saturation & Attention Distortion — PFC Suppression
Multi-channel sensory overload delivered via AR/XR headsets can suppress prefrontal cortex activity, degrading executive function and increasing susceptibility to suggestion. The mechanism — identified in tDCS desynchronisation studies — can be replicated purely through carefully crafted audiovisual stimuli at legal intensities.
HIGH
Perception Modification via tDCS / TMS / Focused Ultrasound
Transcranial direct current stimulation (tDCS), transcranial magnetic stimulation (TMS), and transcranial focused ultrasound (tFUS) are commercially available and, at sub-therapeutic intensities, can produce measurable changes in perception, mood, and decision-making without triggering adverse-event reporting thresholds. Consumer wellness devices increasingly incorporate these modalities without adequate risk disclosure.
MODERATE
Subliminal Priming — Sub-17ms Visual Frames, Acoustic Embedding, LTP
Visual stimuli presented below the conscious perception threshold (<17ms at 60 Hz displays, <8ms at 120 Hz) can induce measurable changes in subsequent decision-making via long-term potentiation (LTP) effects. Similarly, acoustic content embedded in masking noise at sub-threshold intensities has been shown to influence emotional valence. High-refresh-rate AR displays and spatial audio systems create practical delivery vectors.

7.5 — Neuromodulation Hijack

CRITICAL
Implanted Stimulator RF Attacks — DBS/MICS-Band SDR Replay/Injection
Deep brain stimulation (DBS) and other implanted neural stimulators communicate via the Medical Implant Communication Service (MICS) band (402–405 MHz). Researchers have demonstrated that SDR equipment can replay recorded stimulation sequences or inject novel patterns at ranges up to 3 meters, inducing involuntary motor responses or disrupting therapeutic stimulation programs in implanted devices lacking authenticated RF protocols. As of 2025, the majority of active implants do not implement cryptographic authentication of stimulation commands.
CRITICAL
tFUS Covert Modulation — Non-Contact, Through-Wall, Below FDA Threshold
Transcranial focused ultrasound at intensities below the FDA's 720 mW/cm² ISPTA reporting threshold can produce localised cortical excitation or inhibition non-invasively and, with phased-array transducers, through building materials at ranges up to 2 metres. The acoustic penetration depth, spatial precision (<3mm), and below-threshold operation combine to create a covert neuromodulation vector with no reliable detection method available at consumer scale.

7.8 — Neurobiometrics (Extended)

The neurobiometric threat is distinguished from all prior biometric threats by five properties that make it categorically more severe:

Property 1

Irrevocability

ERP component latencies (P300 ~300ms, N200 ~200ms, ERN post-error) are genetically determined and stable across decades. Compromise is permanent.

Property 2

Cross-Platform Re-ID

A neural fingerprint extracted from a consumer headset in 2024 can re-identify the same individual on a clinical BCI in 2030, across all intermediate platforms.

Property 3

Involuntary Disclosure

P300 oddball responses are elicited by surprising stimuli regardless of intent. ERN responses to errors occur below conscious awareness. Consent cannot override physiology.

Property 4

P300 / ERN Capture

Sequences of rapidly presented images (RSVP paradigm at 10 Hz) can elicit P300 responses to recognised faces, locations, or objects — extracting knowledge the subject did not intend to disclose.

Defense

NeuralMask™

EEGShield's adversarial FGSM layer targets the spectral bins used by P300 and ERN fingerprinting pipelines, reducing re-ID accuracy to chance level (<52%) while preserving >94% clinical signal quality.
